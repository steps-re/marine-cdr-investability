# National / Public mCDR Investment vs the Equilibration Filter

## THE WEDGE (most robust, publication-ready finding)
National governments invest in mCDR on **coastline availability, habitat area/tonnage, and capture potential**
-- NOT on realized-atmospheric-removal efficiency (air-sea equilibration geography). The rigor exists only in
(a) peer-reviewed MRV science and (b) private crediting protocols (Isometric), NOT in public decision criteria.
Verified by full-text search: US 2024 National Marine CDR Strategy = 0 hits for "equilibration"/"additionality";
NOAA 2023 sizes macroalgae by coastline strip length; Australia CSIRO Roadmap sizes OAE by desalination proximity,
omits equilibration from "known limitations"; Singapore Equatic omits it explicitly; Japan/Korea/China/Brazil/India
blue-carbon = per-hectare stock, no air-sea term; Chile sites in Humboldt without flagging outgassing.
Frame as NEGATIVE claim precisely: "treated as generic outgassing/permanence loss, not quantified as
location-dependent realized uptake."

## TWO PHYSICS CORRECTIONS the sources force (fix our earlier heuristics)
1. HIGH LATITUDE is favorable for WIND (fast k) but often PENALIZED for OAE by deep winter convection /
   deepwater formation, which lengthens equilibration AND subducts alkalinity before uptake (He & Tyka 2023:
   deepwater-formation regions lose ~half the alkalinity, take 8-10+ yr). So Norway/Iceland-region deepwater
   formation HURTS OAE realized efficiency. "High latitude = good" is WRONG as stated.
2. UPWELLING zones (Humboldt/Chile, equatorial) bring carbon-RICH high-DIC water that OUTGASSES (CO2 SOURCES,
   Takahashi 2009). Generally UNFAVORABLE for a net sink -- OPPOSITE to the earlier "upwelling = good" premise.
   (NOTE: He & Tyka list Peru/Patagonia as kinetically FAST for OAE relaxation; the source/sink + advective-
   resupply effect is a distinct, additional consideration. Defer to published efficiency fields per-location,
   don't trust simple heuristics. Our box-model emulator captures kinetics, NOT advective resupply/source-sink.)
   Iron fertilization INVERTS the logic: deepwater-formation zones HELP OIF permanence (Siegel 2021, Bach 2023).

## Favorability mapping (qualitative, per published physics)
- Favorable: Patagonia/Tasmania/Kerguelen shelves (Chile-south, Australia), high-lat N Pacific/Gulf of Alaska
  shelves (US, Canada), subpolar N Atlantic SINK regions (but deepwater-formation spots penalized for OAE).
- Mixed/penalized: Norway Nordic/Barents (great gas exchange, hurt by convective deepwater formation);
  Australia Southern Ocean margins (high wind + high max, but deep mixing + upwelling lengthen equilibration).
- Unfavorable: subtropical gyres + stratified/low-wind coasts -- US SE, Japan (Kuroshio), EU/Iberia +
  Med/Baltic, Singapore/Gulf/Brazil/India (tropical warm low-wind stratified), Chile Humboldt (outgassing).
- NET: several nations investing in ENGINEERED mCDR (Singapore, Japan, Gulf, tropical Brazil/India, Chile
  upwelling) sit on PHYSICALLY UNFAVORABLE water; best-endowed coasts (Patagonia, high-lat shelves) under-invested.

  strategies (NOAA 2023; OSTP/NSTC National Strategy Nov 2024, 7 pathways, excl. blue carbon+geologic). EPA MPRSA
  permits: WHOI LOC-NESS (NaOH Gulf of Maine Aug 2025) + Carboniferous. Contrast: $3.5B DAC Hubs (not ocean).
- Canada: OFI "Transforming Climate Action" CAD $154M (CFREF 2023, OAE, Bedford Basin); Solid Carbon CAD $24M/6yr
  (DOC + sub-seabed basalt off Vancouver Is.). Planetary Halifax = Tufts Cove power-plant cooling outfall, Mg(OH)2,
  mostly private/philanthropic (Frontier $7M+$31.3M). Canada = cleanest science-vs-policy gap: Planetary MRV
  theoretical ~1300t -> modeled 882t -> credited 626t (~48% haircut); Dalhousie/Fennel ~51.5% realized in Halifax.
- UK: DESNZ DAC&GGR Phase 2 GBP 59.46M/15 proj (only DOC among mCDR; excl. fertilization+nature-based).
  SeaCURE GBP 3.0M (only marine/DOC, Weymouth ~100t/yr). GGR Demonstrators GBP 22.5M all TERRESTRIAL.
  Planetary Cornwall/St Ives trial ABANDONED ("commercial infeasibility").
- Norway: big money = Longship/Northern Lights CCS (geologic, NOT mCDR). Real mCDR = Captura Karsto DOC (Equinor,

- EU: Horizon consortia -- OceanNETs EUR 7.19M (GEOMAR), SEAO2-CDR EUR 4.44M (MRV), RESCUE EUR 8.0M. CRCF marine
  methods "being explored", NO adopted ocean method (say "not yet published", not "rejects").

- Japan: GX ¥20T transition bonds (CCS slice ¥4T = geologic). Moonshot Goal 4 ¥50.1B. Overwhelmingly BLUE CARBON

- New Zealand: MBIE Endeavour NZ$11.02M/5yr "Catching Marine Carbon" (natural analogues, not deployment).

- Australia: CSIRO CDR Roadmap Nov 2025 -- OAE only marine pathway modeled (up to 7 Mt/yr co-located desal

- Singapore: Equatic-1 (PUB+NRF+UCLA, US$20M, Tuas 1.3N, 10 t/day). Legal analysis notes NO discussion of air-sea

- Gulf/Brazil/India: mangrove/seaweed blue carbon only; no funded engineered OAE/DOC.
