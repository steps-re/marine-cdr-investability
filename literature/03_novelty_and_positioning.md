# Novelty Check & Positioning

## Verdict
- Physics: settled, NOT novel (Jones 2014; He & Tyka 2023; Zhou 2024).
- Conceptual framing (air-sea equilibration as cross-pathway constraint): PRE-EMPTED.
  - **Oschlies et al. (2025)** "Perspectives and challenges of marine CDR," *Frontiers in Climate*,
    doi:10.3389/fclim.2024.1506181. Treats slow equilibration as fundamental cross-cutting constraint;
    "airborne fraction smaller than unity." Conceptual only; NO pathway ranking, NO company overlay.
  - **Yamamoto, DeVries & Siegel (2024)** *ERL* 19(10):104053, doi:10.1088/1748-9326/ad7477.
    Defines cumulative additionality alpha and relative efficiency epsilon. OAE alpha starts at 0, ->40-90%
    over years-decades; epsilon->~100% only after ~40 yr. Covers OAE vs DAC only; NO biological/iron; NO site map.
  - **Hurd et al. (2024)** *J. Phycology*, doi:10.1111/jpy.13405. Sinking Sargassum = 6-33% of nominal CDR
    after equilibration. Makes storage-vs-coupled point for SEAWEED only, not as general framing.

## OPEN novel contribution (the paper)
1. EMPIRICAL OVERLAY: real mCDR company/project deployment geographies x published efficiency fields
   (Zhou 2024 / [C]Worthy OAE Efficiency Atlas source.coop/cworthy/oae-efficiency-atlas + CarbonPlan explainers),
   benchmarking whether the industry systematically sites into SLOW-equilibration regimes. NOT in the literature.
2. Extend alpha/epsilon framing across the FULL pathway set (add biological export + iron fertilization).
3. Formalize STORAGE-ONLY vs ATMOSPHERE-COUPLED dichotomy as the organizing axis (terrestrial biomass sinking
   removes atmospheric C at photosynthesis, NOT air-sea gated; all marine-grown/ocean-chemistry pathways ARE).
